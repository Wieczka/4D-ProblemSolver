ReGrow Pack — biodegradable protective packaging

Prepared from assigned inventive problem · Contradiction TRIZ-14 vs TRIZ-31

Problem

{{ problemText }}

Technical contradiction

Improving strength (TRIZ-14) — the durability and moisture resistance packaging needs in transit — currently worsens object-generated harmful factors (TRIZ-31): the composite layers responsible for that toughness persist as waste after use.

Proposed concept

A molded shell of pressed agricultural fibre (straw / husk pulp), bound by grown mycelium and sealed with a thin chitosan-nanocrystal moisture barrier. Rigid and water-resistant while dry and in transit — satisfying TRIZ-14 — then fully home-compostable or re-pulpable into new packaging stock once wetted, resolving TRIZ-31 without a plastic-film liner.

This applies principles 40 (composite materials), 35 (parameter changes) and 10 (preliminary action) from the prior step: the barrier is engineered to hold during shipping and release on contact with moisture or industrial compost heat, rather than requiring a separate disposal step.
